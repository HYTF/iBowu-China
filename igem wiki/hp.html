    PREVIOUS MARKETING RESEARCH (Jan.-May)

    1) Online Survey About Potato Consumptions And Soft Rot

    We have conducted a survey on potato consumptions and soft rot disease, with a duration of two weeks during the previous market research of our project design. We received 181 responses in total for the questions related to potato consumptions and 14 responses from potato farmers for the questions related to potato farming. From the results, we are able to conclude that potatoes play significant roles in people's diet, but most people are not aware of the issue of soft rot disease. In addition, it has been reported that the current drugs for soft rot diseases are not effective enough or have certain problems like broad-spectrum bactericide; therefore, it is important for the public to develop a more efficient drug curing potato soft rot. Furthermore, according to the responses from some large-scale potato farm owners, soft rot is not a very serious problem in potato farming in northern China, so our team might also need to focus on blackleg and other bacterial diseases that infect potatoes.

    Event Description

    On July 7th of 2019, our team, iBowu China along with team SDSZ_China held the 2019 iGEM Beijing District Meeting at Songshan Art Zone. From the early stage of planning, finding sponsors, invitation of teams to conference details, and schedule planning, we gave in our best efforts as well as large amounts of time to make this to an amazing experience for the participating teams, and it eventually payed off; in total, 11 teams partook in this meeting, which consists of 8 high school teams and 3 university teams from Beijing, Wuxi and Shanghai. All the teams had the opportunity to present their projects, learn new knowledge, deliver concerns as well as seek for collaborations and sponsorships during the conference. Some of the teams including us entered iGEM for the very first time, thus, this forum gave us the best occasion to learn from the previous participators together with the experts. Furthermore, we were very lucky to have the iGEM Asia Ambassador, Ms. Zhangnan along with the After iGEM High School Committee chair Jeff Hu to introduce us to After iGEM as well as giving each group feedbacks after their presentations for further improvements. After 7 hours of intense discussions and project presentations, the conference ended with great success.

    Special Interview with Haiyong Wang

    Mr. Haiyong Wang is the deputy director of the Health Risk Assessment and Control Committee of the Chinese Preventive Medicine Association and the Director of the Public Relations Department of the Beijing Municipal Medical Examination Center. The main content of this interview is to discuss the health value of potato instead of polished grains and the significance of potatoes being staple foods. This interview was carried out by Linda Liu (regional team leader of the US Areas). From this interview, we concluded the followings: 1) Potatoes are also high-nutrient and low-energy foods that has a good effect on the treatment of diabetes and weight loss. 2) The benefits of potatoes have not yet been discovered by the general public. 3) Consuming large doses of potatoes infected by soft rot disease can cause serious health issue and likely increase the risk of carcinogenicity. According to this interview, we can infer that potato has many health benefits, and it is necessary to wipe out soft rot disease by utilizing synthetic biology.

    PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT

    1) School Lecture

    We held an interactive lecture at Beijing Primary School facing grade 6 students. In the lecture, we discussed the concepts of synthetic biology, including the composition of genes and basic biological knowledges. We intend to make biology an easy and understandable subject that is simple enough to be understood as sixth-grade students. From a scientific prospective, we showed them how the GE process is done and updated the latest research in this field. Through discussions and debates, some students were able to form a preliminary opinion on GE crops. We hope that through spreading the knowledges of synthetic biology to the younger generations, we can have more students who are willing to be involved in iGEM in the future.
